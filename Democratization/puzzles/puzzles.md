Here are the 10 most important puzzles in the scientific study of democratization, framed as conflicts between "Common Sense" and "The Data."

### 1. The Modernization Puzzle: Why doesn't wealth equate to freedom?
* **The Puzzle:** Common sense suggests that as a country gets richer (like China or Saudi Arabia), it will inevitably become democratic. But the data shows this relationship is broken.
* **The Twist:** Adam Przeworski’s research reveals that wealth *never* causes a transition to democracy. It only prevents democracies from dying once they exist. The puzzle is why money acts as a "shield" for democracy but not a "sword" against dictatorship.

### 2. The Resource Puzzle: Why is free money a curse?
* **The Puzzle:** If a country discovers massive oil reserves, the government should have more money to build schools, hospitals, and happy citizens. Yet, these countries almost never democratize.
* **The Twist:** The "Rentier State" theory (Michael Ross). Democracy is born from a negotiation: citizens agree to pay taxes in exchange for representation. If the state doesn't need your taxes (because it has oil), it doesn't need to give you rights. The puzzle is how "good luck" geologically becomes "bad luck" politically.

### 3. The Military Puzzle: Why do men with guns obey men without them?
* **The Puzzle:** In every transition, the military has the physical power to crush the opposition. Why do they ever voluntarily go back to the barracks and take orders from civilians?
* **The Twist:** This is the puzzle of "Civil-Military Relations." The military often supports democratization *not* because they love freedom, but to protect their own corporate interests (budget, prestige) from a reckless dictator who is ruining the country.

### 4. The "Bourgeoisie" Puzzle: Is the middle class actually democratic?
* **The Puzzle:** Since Aristotle, we’ve believed that a strong middle class is the backbone of democracy.
* **The Twist:** History shows the middle class often supports military coups (e.g., Thailand, Chile in 1973, Egypt in 2013). The puzzle is that the middle class is only democratic when they fear the dictator *more* than they fear the poor. If they fear the poor (redistribution) too much, they will hug the dictator.

### 5. The Violence Puzzle: Why are unarmed rebels more dangerous?
* **The Puzzle:** Intuitively, if you want to overthrow a brutal regime, you need weapons to fight back.
* **The Twist:** Erica Chenoweth’s data shows that non-violent campaigns are twice as likely to succeed as violent insurgencies. The puzzle is how "weakness" (refusing to fight) becomes a strategic strength by encouraging security forces to defect.


### 6. The Election Puzzle: Why do dictators hold elections they might lose?
* **The Puzzle:** Why would a dictator like Putin or Maduro bother with the expense and risk of holding an election? Why not just rule by decree?
* **The Twist:** "Electoral Authoritarianism." Dictators use elections not to let people choose, but to gather information on who is loyal and to distribute patronage. The puzzle is how these sham elections sometimes accidentally trigger real democratization (the "Stunning Election" phenomenon).

### 7. The Institutional Puzzle: Why do we copy the wrong constitution?
* **The Puzzle:** When new democracies are born, they often copy the US system (Presidentialism).
* **The Twist:** Juan Linz’s research shows that Presidential systems are statistically much more likely to collapse into civil war or dictatorship than Parliamentary systems. The puzzle is why new nations keep choosing a system that is structurally prone to gridlock and failure.

### 8. The "Arab" Puzzle: Is it Culture or Geology?
* **The Puzzle:** For a long time, the Middle East was the only region resisting the global wave of democratization. Many blamed Islamic culture ("Clash of Civilizations").
* **The Twist:** More rigorous analysis suggests it has nothing to do with religion and everything to do with oil and the military apparatus. If you control for oil wealth, Muslim-majority countries are no less democratic than anyone else.

### 9. The Redistribution Puzzle: Why doesn't democracy level the playing field?
* **The Puzzle:** The Median Voter Theorem suggests that in a democracy, the majority (who are poor) should vote to tax the rich. Therefore, democracies should rapidly reduce inequality.
* **The Twist:** In reality, many new democracies see inequality *rise*. The puzzle is "State Capture"—how elites learn to control the democratic process (lobbying, media control) so effectively that the vote no longer threatens their wealth.

### 10. The Sequencing Puzzle: Order first or Freedom first?
* **The Puzzle:** Should you hold elections immediately after a dictator falls?
* **The Twist:** "The Sequencing Debate" (Sheri Berman vs. Jack Snyder). If you hold elections before you have a functioning police force and bureaucracy, you get violence (Iraq, Libya). But if you wait to build the state first, the interim leader never leaves. The puzzle is how to build the ship (state) while sailing it (democracy).
